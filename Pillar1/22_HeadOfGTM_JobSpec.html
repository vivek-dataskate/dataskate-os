Head of GTM

Full-time · Pillar 1 — Partner Alliance & GTM · Reports to VP of Alliances · Manages SDR/BDR
Full GTM ownership — inbound + outbound Manages SDR/BDR direct report First hire: Head of GTM · SDR joins 6–8 weeks later $75–95K USD · AUD $95–125K
Context
Why This Role Exists Now
The problem this role solves
DataSkate's revenue engine has been entirely inbound — waiting for MuleSoft and Salesforce AEs to refer prospects. That model works but it caps growth at the AE's referral willingness. The Head of GTM builds and runs the outbound motion that makes DataSkate the demand generation engine for partner AEs — not just a recipient of their referrals. In parallel, they run the inbound content and brand machine that keeps DataSkate visible and trusted in the Salesforce/MuleSoft ecosystem.

VP of Alliances should be closing deals and managing partner relationships — not managing SDR sequences or reviewing content calendars. Head of GTM owns all of that, runs it independently, and reports outcomes weekly.
What success looks like at 6 months
SDR hired, onboarded, and booking 4–6 qualified 3-way calls per week
Outbound pipeline contributing ≥30% of total qualified leads
CEO LinkedIn at 3,000+ followers with consistent engagement
Weekly GTM KPI report running without VP Alliances asking for it
Full sequence library — 4 pain bucket variants, tested and iterated
VP Alliances is not managing SDR or content day-to-day
What this role is NOT
A content coordinator who takes briefs — this role sets the strategy
A demand gen specialist who ignores content — both sides are owned
A sales role — no discovery calls, no SOW scoping, no closing
A brand/social media manager — content serves pipeline, not vanity metrics
A junior role — managing a direct report (SDR) and owning pipeline outcomes requires seniority

Hiring Profile
Who You're Looking For
The rare combination — and why it matters
Most marketers are strong on one side: content people who understand brand and storytelling, or demand gen people who understand outbound and pipeline. This role needs both. The hire must have genuinely run cold email campaigns — not just "I know what Instantly is" — AND have produced B2B content that drove engagement. Background to look for: demand gen or growth marketing at a B2B SaaS or SI company, 4–6 years experience, with direct evidence of both.
Must-haves Non-negotiable
4–6 years B2B marketing experience — not agency, not B2C
Has personally written and run cold email sequences — knows deliverability, reply rate, A/B testing
Has used or can immediately learn: Apollo, Clay, Instantly (or equivalent outbound stack)
Has produced B2B content — LinkedIn posts, case studies, collateral — that got engagement and drove pipeline
Has managed at least one direct report or contractor — not a first-time people manager
Comfortable reporting pipeline metrics to a revenue leader — not just vanity metrics
Understands the Salesforce / MuleSoft ecosystem — knows what an SI is, what a partner AE does, why co-sell matters
Strong-to-haves High value
Background at a Salesforce SI, consulting firm, or SaaS company selling to enterprise IT
Has built a personal brand or exec LinkedIn presence — knows what makes content work on LinkedIn
HubSpot experience — can build reports, set up attribution, maintain pipeline hygiene
Experience with Shield App, Buffer/Taplio, Canva, or equivalent content tools
Has been part of a company scaling from $1M to $5M ARR — understands the scrappiness required
Has written objection guides or sales enablement material — understands the sales motion, not just marketing
Immediate disqualifiers — do not progress
Pure content background with no outbound or demand gen experience — will build good content but sequences will fail
Pure outbound/sales background with no content experience — will run decent sequences but brand and collateral will be generic and weak
Never managed a direct report — SDR management requires people leadership experience
Only measured themselves on impressions and followers — needs to be accountable to pipeline metrics
Expects a large team or big budget — DataSkate at this stage is lean; this role must do the work, not direct others to do it
Interview Guide
Interview Questions — What to Ask and What to Listen For
1. "Walk me through a cold email sequence you wrote and ran. What was the pain bucket, what were the open and reply rates, and what did you change after the first 50 sends?"
Why: Tests real outbound execution experience. Anyone who has done this will have specific numbers and specific changes. Anyone who hasn't will give vague answers about "personalisation" without data.
Listen for: Specific metrics (open rate %, reply rate %), a named tool (Instantly, Outreach, etc.), a specific sequence structure, and evidence of iteration based on data — not just writing it once and walking away.
2. "Tell me about a piece of B2B content you produced that drove pipeline. Not impressions — actual pipeline. How do you know it worked?"
Why: Tests whether they tie content to revenue. A strong candidate connects content to HubSpot UTM tracking, to inbound requests, to conversations started. A weak candidate talks about impressions and engagement without linking to pipeline.
Listen for: Attribution methodology, specific deal or lead that came from the content, willingness to admit when content didn't convert despite good engagement.
3. "How would you ICP a list of 500 companies for a MuleSoft implementation partner? Walk me through the filters and priority ranking."
Why: Tests whether they understand the Salesforce/MuleSoft ecosystem and ICP thinking. Tests Apollo/Clay knowledge. Tests whether they think in terms of intent signals, not just firmographics.
Listen for: Tech stack detection (MuleSoft/Salesforce users), integration job postings as intent signal, company size logic, title prioritisation (VP IT vs Developer), mention of Clay or Apollo enrichment, scoring logic.
4. "You have an SDR who's been in the role 6 weeks. They're hitting 15 dials a day but only booking 1 call per week. What do you do?"
Why: Tests SDR management and diagnostic thinking. The problem could be the ICP, the sequence, the qualifying threshold, the call script, or the prospect list quality. A good Head of GTM knows how to diagnose which one.
Listen for: Structured diagnostic approach — "first I'd look at X, then Y." Red flag: immediately jumping to "we need to change the sequences" without diagnosing. Green flag: checking the prospect list quality and ICP fit first.
5. "You're 30 days in. Walk me through exactly what you'd build first, and why in that order."
Why: Tests prioritisation thinking and understanding of the system dependency chain. The right order is: ICP → domain warmup → sequence library → test personally → SDR hired into working system. Wrong order: hire SDR first, then figure out ICP.
Listen for: Domain warmup in the first 30 days (critical — 3 weeks minimum before sequences run). ICP definition before sequences. Testing personally before handing to SDR. Structured sequence library before SDR joins.
6. "How do you report GTM performance to a revenue leader who doesn't want to hear about impressions?"
Why: Tests whether they can translate marketing activity to revenue language. VP Alliances cares about pipeline, calls booked, deals influenced — not reach and engagement.
Listen for: Pipeline by motion, cost per booked call, conversion rate from sequence reply to qualified call, deal influence attribution. Red flag: talking about follower growth and post impressions as primary metrics.
Onboarding Plan
30 / 60 / 90 Day Plan
Days 1–30 · Learn & Design
  • Shadow 3+ discovery calls with Alliance Mgr/AE — hear what pain prospects articulate in their own words
  • Interview VP Alliances on ICP — which companies close fastest, which titles sign, which pain creates urgency
  • Audit existing content, collateral, and sequences — document what exists and what's missing
  • Set up tool stack — Apollo, Clay, Instantly, Shield, Buffer/Taplio all live and configured
  • Start domain warmup — minimum 3 weeks before any cold sequences launch
  • Take over CEO content workflow — understand the approval process, start producing posts
  • End of Day 30: ICP documented, tool stack live, domain warmup running, CEO content cadence active
Days 31–60 · Build & Test
  • Write full 9-touch sequence library — 4 pain bucket variants, tested for tone and personalisation hooks
  • Build first prospect list — 200 contacts enriched via Clay, emails verified, bounce rate checked
  • Launch test sequences personally — Head of GTM sends first batch before SDR joins
  • Iterate based on first open/reply data — rewrite any step with open rate below 30%
  • Produce SDR onboarding pack — ICP doc, sequence playbook, qualifying call script, objection guide, tool walkthroughs
  • Begin SDR hiring process — job posting live, first interviews in progress
  • End of Day 60: Sequences tested and iterated, first replies received, SDR hired or offer out
Days 61–90 · SDR Joins & Reports
  • SDR onboarded into a working system — not building from scratch
  • SDR shadows Head of GTM on outreach for first 2 weeks before going solo
  • Weekly SDR review established — Monday 30 min, fixed agenda, data-driven
  • First booked 3-way calls — SDR targets 2/week in ramp month 1
  • Weekly GTM KPI report running every Friday without VP Alliances requesting it
  • First monthly GTM performance review presented to VP Alliances
  • End of Day 90: SDR booking calls, outbound pipeline visible in HubSpot, VP Alliances not managing SDR day-to-day
Compensation
Salary Range & What Drives It
Why this role commands a higher salary than a coordinator
This is not a GTM Coordinator with extra responsibilities. It is a Head of GTM who owns strategy, manages a direct report, and is accountable for pipeline outcomes. The rare combination of outbound expertise and content capability at this seniority level is priced accordingly in the market. Underpaying gets you a coordinator who does the easy parts and avoids the hard ones.
