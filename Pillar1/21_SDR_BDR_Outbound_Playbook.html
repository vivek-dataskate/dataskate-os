Section 01
SDR/BDR Role — Mission, Scope & What This Is NOT
DataSkate doesn't wait for partner AEs to refer leads. DataSkate finds prospects, runs aggressive multi-touch outreach, gets them interested in MuleSoft/Salesforce implementation, then hands a warm, qualified, interested prospect directly to the partner AE. The SDR makes the AE's quota easier. That is the value exchange.
The mindset shift — DataSkate is the demand generation engine FOR the partner AE
Old model: Wait for MuleSoft AE to refer a lead → respond within 24hrs → close.
New model: DataSkate SDR finds prospect → runs outreach → gets prospect interested → books 3-way call with MuleSoft AE → AE closes with DataSkate as SI.

The MuleSoft AE now has a reason to prioritise DataSkate above every other SI — because DataSkate is bringing THEM pipeline. This is the most powerful partner position possible.
What the SDR/BDR Owns
Prospect list building — Apollo, Clay, LinkedIn Sales Nav
Email sequence writing and A/B testing — Instantly
Sending all outbound emails, LinkedIn DMs, cold calls
Qualifying interest — BANT lite before booking
Booking the discovery call for Alliance Mgr/AE + Partner AE

Section 03
Ideal Customer Profile — Who to Target and Why
Specific enough to write a personalised email. Broad enough to have 10,000+ companies in the addressable universe.
Company Firmographics
  • Size: 200–5,000 employees (SMB-MM sweet spot)
  • Revenue: $20M–$1B ARR
  • Industry: Financial services, healthcare, retail, logistics, SaaS, manufacturing — any company with complex system integration needs
  • Location: US primary · ANZ/UK secondary based on AE territory
  • Structure: Has an IT/Engineering team but not big enough to build integrations in-house at scale
Target Titles (Decision Makers)
  • Primary: VP of IT, Director of IT, Head of Engineering
  • Primary: CTO, CIO at SMB (<500 employees)
  • Primary: Director/VP of Integrations or Architecture
  • Secondary: Head of Digital Transformation
  • Secondary: Salesforce Admin/Architect (for referral to decision maker)
  • Avoid: Junior IT staff, pure developers — they don't sign SOWs
Tech Stack Signals (Apollo/Clay)
Strong buy signals — target immediately:
MuleSoft detected Salesforce CRM user Informatica Workato Boomi

Good signals — likely integration pain:
SAP + Salesforce Oracle + Salesforce Multiple SaaS tools AWS/Azure + SFDC

Intent signals:
Hiring integration engineer Digital transformation project Recently funded
Pain Points We Target
  • Systems not talking to each other — data silos between Salesforce, ERP, and other tools
  • Manual data entry between platforms — staff doing what automation should do
  • MuleSoft/Salesforce purchased but not properly implemented — licence waste
  • Failed or stalled integration project — previous vendor let them down
  • Scaling fast but integrations can't keep up — engineering team overwhelmed
  • Compliance-driven integration requirements (HIPAA, GDPR, SOX)
Buying Triggers — When to Reach Out
  • Company just raised a funding round (Series A/B) — growth = integration pain
  • New CTO/CIO/VP IT hire — first 90 days, wants to make an impact
  • Job posting for MuleSoft/Salesforce developer — they need help NOW
  • Company went through M&A — always creates integration chaos
  • New Salesforce licence signed — they need someone to implement it
  • MuleSoft contract renewal coming up — do they want to get more value first?
Bad Fit — Do NOT Contact
  • Companies below 50 employees — too small, can't afford SOW minimums
  • Companies with 50+ in-house integration engineers — they build internally
  • Pure Microsoft stack with no Salesforce/MuleSoft — wrong platform
  • Government / heavily regulated without procurement processes understood
  • Companies currently with another SI in active engagement — respect the relationship
  • Anyone that says "we have a preferred vendor" — move on, not worth fighting
Section 04
Prospect Sourcing — Building the List Weekly
SDR builds and refreshes the prospect list every week. Two sources: partner AE named accounts (highest priority) and self-sourced via Apollo/Clay (volume engine).
Source 1 — Partner AE Named Accounts (Priority)
VP Alliances asks MuleSoft AE: "Share your top 20 named accounts you're trying to get into"
SDR finds the right contact titles at those companies via Apollo/LinkedIn
Personalises outreach specifically to that company + their known tech stack
Email references the MuleSoft relationship early: "We partner with your MuleSoft team..."
Conversion rate: 3–5x higher than cold self-sourced. Always do these first.
Source 2 — Self-Sourced via Apollo + Clay (Volume)
Apollo filters: tech stack includes Salesforce/MuleSoft + 200–5000 employees + US + target titles
Clay enriches: pulls job postings, funding data, tech stack signals, LinkedIn activity
Prioritise by intent signal: hiring MuleSoft dev > recent funding > new exec hire
60 net-new prospects per week minimum into Instantly sequences
Verify emails in Apollo before loading — bounce rate must stay below 3%
Clay enrichment workflow — the difference between a generic blast and a personalised sequence
Apollo gives you the list. Clay makes it personal. Use Clay to pull: (1) recent LinkedIn posts by the prospect — what are they talking about? (2) Job postings — are they hiring integration engineers? (3) Tech stack — exactly which platforms are they running? (4) Company news — funding, M&A, new product launches. Feed this into the first line of every email. "I saw [Company] just posted a MuleSoft developer role — we help companies like yours shortcut that hire entirely." That level of personalisation gets 3–4x the reply rate of generic outreach.

Objection Handling — What They Say and Exactly What to Respond
Every objection is a buying signal. None of these are dead ends — they're the conversation starting.
What they sayWhat it actually meansWhat SDR says backGoal
"We already have an SI partner" They're in a relationship but are they happy? Is the current SI delivering? "Totally understand — most companies we work with had one before us. Are you happy with what you're getting? If yes, great. If you've got any frustrations, that's worth a 15-min conversation." Plant a seed. Don't push. Ask to stay in touch for the renewal.
"We handle integration internally" They have engineers but are probably backlogged or under-resourced "That's actually our most common client — they have an internal team but the queue is always 3 months long. We plug in as an extension, not a replacement. Does your team ever struggle with capacity?" Reframe — not replacing them, extending capacity
"We don't have budget right now" Not a priority, or budget cycle is wrong time "When does your budget cycle reset? We're happy to be a conversation for Q[X]. What I can do is send you something concrete so when the time comes you've already seen what we do — is that OK?" Get into the next budget cycle. Stay in nurture.
"We're not looking at MuleSoft right now" May not understand what DataSkate does, or not in an active project "That's fine — we also do Salesforce, Boomi, and Workato implementations. Is any integration project active at the moment, regardless of platform?" Broaden the platform angle — it's not just MuleSoft
"Send me some information" Polite way to end the call — or genuine interest if tone is right "Happy to — what's the specific thing most relevant to you right now? I want to send you exactly the right thing, not a brochure." [Then send a 1-pager + case study within 1 hour, with a follow-up booked] Qualify before sending. Book the follow-up before hanging up.
"We're in the middle of a project already" Active project = active decision making = perfect time "That's actually the best time to talk — if the project is already running, we can often come in and accelerate it or fix what's not working. Who's running it for you?" Get into the active project — even as a second vendor
"I'm not the right person" Genuine — or a brush-off. Ask before accepting it. "I appreciate that — who would be the right person for a conversation about [platform] implementation? I can reach out to them directly and let them know you suggested it." Get the right name. Use their name as a referral intro.
Section 07
Pre-Booking Qualifying Call Script — 5 Questions, Under 10 Minutes
When a prospect replies with interest, the SDR runs this call before booking the 3-way. Goal: find out which pain bucket they're in so the Alliance Mgr/AE and MuleSoft AE walk into the call already knowing what to say. A 3-way call with no context is a waste of everyone's time.
The 4 pain buckets — every prospect lands in one of these
Bucket A — "Just bought, not implemented": They have a MuleSoft/Salesforce licence sitting unused or partially deployed. Licence waste is the pain. Speed is the pitch.
Bucket B — "Implemented badly": A previous SI or internal team built something that's broken, slow, or unmaintainable. Rescue and rebuild is the pitch.
Bucket C — "Systems don't talk": They have Salesforce + ERP/other tools that aren't integrated. Data silos, manual work, reporting errors. Integration build is the pitch.
Bucket D — "Scaling fast, can't keep up": Engineering team is overwhelmed, backlog is months long, integrations are blocking product/ops velocity. Capacity extension is the pitch.

The qualifying call identifies the bucket. The brief tells the AEs which bucket before they join the 3-way.
Call Setup — Before You Dial
Pull up their Clay enrichment — tech stack, company size, any job postings, recent news
Check their LinkedIn profile — recent posts, tenure in role, any changes at the company
Open HubSpot contact — log the call in real time
Have the Notion brief template open — fill it as they talk
Target call length: 8–12 minutes. Not a discovery — that's the AE's job. This is intake only.
Opening Line — First 20 Seconds
"Thanks for making time, [Name] — I want to keep this quick. I have a few short questions so I can bring in the right people from our side and make sure this call is actually worth your hour. Sound good?"
Why it works: sets the agenda, positions the SDR as organised, frames the 3-way call as high-value, and respects their time.
They almost always say yes — it implies you're being selective about who you bring in.
The 5 Questions
Q1
Question 1 Situation What are they actually running today
"Which platforms are you currently running — Salesforce, MuleSoft, anything else in the integration stack?"
What you're listening for:
— Do they have MuleSoft already? → Likely Bucket A or B
— Do they have Salesforce but NOT MuleSoft? → Likely Bucket C, potential net-new platform sale
— Do they have both but mention other tools (SAP, Oracle, Workday)? → Likely Bucket C or D
— Do they say "we're evaluating MuleSoft"? → AE needs to be on the 3-way to drive the licence conversation

Probe if needed: "And is that fully deployed or still being rolled out?"
Write exact platforms into the brief. "They run Salesforce Sales Cloud, MuleSoft Anypoint (partially deployed), and SAP S/4HANA" is what the AE needs — not "they have Salesforce."
Q2
Question 2 Pain What's actually broken or missing
"What's the specific problem you're trying to solve — or what made you reply to my email?"
What you're listening for:
— "It's not working / was built badly" → Bucket B (rescue)
— "We bought it but haven't implemented it" → Bucket A (deployment)
— "Our systems don't talk to each other" → Bucket C (integration build)
— "My team is overwhelmed / we can't scale" → Bucket D (capacity)
— "We're evaluating options" → not yet a pain — probe harder

Follow-up if vague: "Can you give me a specific example — what broke last week, or what can't you do today that you need to be able to do?"

If they say "just exploring": "Totally fine — what triggered the exploration? Something must have come up." Keep pushing gently. There's always a trigger.
Get their exact words. Quote them verbatim in the brief. "We have 4 systems that don't talk to each other and our ops team is doing manual exports every morning" is gold for the AE's opening line.
Q3
Question 3 Timeline Is this real and urgent or just exploring
"Is there a timeline you're working to — or a deadline that's driving this?"
What you're listening for:
— "We need this done by [date]" → Hot. Flag to VP Alliances immediately. Move fast.
— "We're trying to get something in place this quarter" → Warm. Standard pipeline.
— "No hard deadline, just exploring" → Lower urgency. Still worth the call but manage expectations with AE.
— "We have a board review / audit / go-live in [X weeks]" → Urgent. Escalate to VP Alliances.

If no urgency stated: "What would have to happen for this to become urgent?" This surfaces the real trigger even when they haven't articulated it yet.
Timeline directly affects whether the Alliance Mgr/AE needs to be on the call this week or can schedule for next week. Always capture this in the brief.
Q4
Question 4 Authority Are we talking to the right person
"When you bring in an outside partner for something like this — who else is typically involved in that decision?"
Why this phrasing, not "are you the decision maker":
Asking "are you the decision maker" makes people defensive. This version invites them to describe the process, which tells you everything.

What you're listening for:
— "Mostly me with sign-off from my CTO/CFO" → They're the champion. Get them on the 3-way. CEO/CFO comes later.
— "I'd need to loop in [Name/Title]" → There's a second decision maker. Ask: "Would it make sense to have them on the call too?"
— "Procurement handles vendor selection" → There's a formal process. Ask: "Have you already been through vendor selection or is this early stage?"
— "I'm just researching for my boss" → They're not the buyer. Ask to be connected to the right person directly.

If they're not the buyer: "Would you be comfortable making an intro to [name/title]? I can prep a short brief for them so it's easy for you to forward."
The 3-way call only works if the right person is in the room. A call with someone who can't influence the decision wastes the MuleSoft AE's time — and that damages the relationship.
Q5
Question 5 Expectation Set Frame the 3-way call before booking it
"Last question — I want to bring our alliance director and your MuleSoft account team on the call. Does that work for you?"
How to explain the MuleSoft AE's presence if they ask:
"We work alongside the MuleSoft team — they can speak to what's available through the partnership, any resources or commercial flexibility. It means you get two conversations in one. They're not there to sell you — they're there because they work with us on these implementations."

If they push back on the MuleSoft AE joining:
"Totally fine — we can start with just our team. If it makes sense after that call to loop them in, we can do that separately."
→ Book without the AE. Tell VP Alliances immediately. They'll decide whether to push for a 3-way on the next call.

Once they confirm:
"Great — let me send you a calendar invite. We'll keep it to 30 minutes. I'll send you a short note beforehand so you know exactly who's joining and what we'll cover."

Then send Calendly link immediately — do not let them leave the call without a time confirmed.
This question does three things: (1) books the call, (2) introduces the MuleSoft AE naturally so they're not a surprise, (3) gives the prospect an easy out if they're uncomfortable — which builds trust and almost always makes them more comfortable.
Prospects who do the qualifying call: 85%+ convert to a booked 3-way
