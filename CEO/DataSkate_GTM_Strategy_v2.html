Motion 1 — MuleSoft AE Referral Program
You already have relationships with MuleSoft AEs. This motion converts those relationships into a structured referral pipeline. Existing trust is the asset — turn it into a repeatable ask.
Why MuleSoft AEs refer — and why they don't
They WILL refer when:
→ You've already delivered cleanly for one of their clients
→ You make them look good in front of their client
→ The deal is too small for them to touch alone
→ The client needs integration work before a MuleSoft renewal
→ You bring a prospect they didn't know about
They WON'T refer when:
→ They don't know what you actually deliver
→ They fear you'll embarrass them with their client
→ You've followed up too many times with no value
→ They don't see any win for themselves
→ You've never delivered anything they can point to
Step 1 — Segment your AE list
Tier every MuleSoft AE you know
Not all AEs are equal. Segment before you reach out — waste no time on tier C.
A
Tier A — AEs you've worked a deal with or met 3+ times. Direct ask for referral. Target: 2 warm intros this month.
B
Tier B — AEs you know but haven't co-sold with. Warm up with value first (webinar, prospect card), then ask in 3–4 weeks.
C
Tier C — Cold contacts on your list. Do not cold-ask for referrals. Route through Motion 2 or 3 instead.
Owner: Alliance Mgr/AE Week 1
Step 2 — The referral ask
One email. One ask. Not a pitch.
The ask must be specific, easy to answer, and show you've done homework.
1
Send a 4-line email: "I know you're working [X type of account]. I've attached a one-pager on what we did for [similar client]. Do you have 2 accounts I could help close for you?"
2
Attach: a single-page client result card, not a deck. Make it co-brandable so the AE can forward it as their own resource.
3
One follow-up only, 5 days later. No more. If no reply, move to Tier B treatment.
Owner: Alliance Mgr/AE Week 1–2
Step 3 — Handle the intro
AE makes the intro — you own everything after
The moment an AE makes an intro, you must make them look good immediately.
1
Respond to prospect within 4 hours of intro email. Not 24. 4 hours. AE is watching.
2
Send the AE a deal status update every week. 3 lines. What happened, what's next, any risk. Never make the AE chase you for status.
3
At deal close: get a client reference quote and send it to the AE before they ask. Register the deal in the partner portal same day — do it for them.
Owner: Alliance Mgr/AE Ongoing
Step 4 — Lock the cadence
Bi-weekly pipeline sync with every Tier A AE
Turn ad-hoc referrals into a structured pipeline exchange.
1
Lock a 30-min bi-weekly call per Tier A AE. Fixed agenda: AE shares 3 accounts, DS shares 3 pre-researched prospects. No other agenda.
2
Send a "monthly deal report" to each Tier A AE — what we ran together, revenue generated, client quote. Makes the AE look good to their own manager.
3
After first closed deal: nominate for partner co-marketing or case study. Elevates AE's visibility inside MuleSoft.
Owner: VP Alliances Month 2+

Motion 2 — SDR/BDR Aggressive Cold Outbound ★ New motion
DataSkate does not wait for AEs to refer leads. The SDR/BDR proactively finds prospects, runs a 9-touch 18-day sequence, qualifies interest, and books a 3-way discovery call: prospect + DataSkate Alliance Mgr/AE + MuleSoft/partner AE. DataSkate generates pipeline for the AE — making DataSkate indispensable to every AE we work with.
Inbound vs Outbound — both motions run in parallel
Inbound motions (Motions 1, 4, 5)
✓ AE referrals — AE introduces DataSkate to their client
✓ Events — warm conversations from attending / speaking
✓ Webinar library — prospects self-identify via gated content
Owner: Alliance Mgr/AE + GTM Manager
Lead quality: High trust · Slower volume
Speed to close: Fast — warm relationship already exists
Outbound motions (Motions 2, 3)
✓ SDR cold outbound — DataSkate finds and contacts prospects directly
✓ AE named accounts — SDR works AE's target list proactively
Owner: SDR/BDR · Reports to VP Alliances
Lead quality: Lower initial trust · Higher volume
Speed to close: Longer — prospect starts cold, warms through sequence
Step 1 — ICP & List Build
SDR builds prospect list weekly — 60+ contacts
Two sources run in parallel. Partner AE named accounts are highest priority (3–5x conversion). Self-sourced via Apollo/Clay is the volume engine.
A
AE-sourced: VP Alliances asks each active MuleSoft AE for 20 named accounts. SDR finds right contacts (VP IT, CTO, Dir Integrations) at those companies via Apollo + LinkedIn Sales Nav. These go into a priority sequence.
B
Self-sourced: Apollo filters — tech stack includes MuleSoft/Salesforce, 200–5,000 employees, US. Clay enriches with job postings, funding signals, LinkedIn activity. Intent signals prioritised: hiring MuleSoft dev, recently funded, new exec hire.
C
Email verification: Verify all emails in Apollo before loading to Instantly. Bounce rate must stay below 3% — above this kills domain reputation and all emails land in spam.
Owner: SDR/BDR Weekly · Monday
Step 2 — 9-Touch Sequence (18 days)
Multi-channel outreach — email, LinkedIn, cold call
Every touch has one job: get a reply or a booked call. Emails are under 100 words. Never educate — interrupt, hook, ask.
D1
Email 1: Pattern interrupt. "Are you getting ROI from [platform]?" Personalised first line from Clay enrichment — company-specific detail, not generic.
D2
LinkedIn: Connection request — no pitch. Just connect. Message comes after acceptance.
D4
Email 2: Case study hook. Industry-matched win — "similar company cut integration time by 60%."
D6
Cold call: 30-second opener referencing emails. If no answer — leave 20-second voicemail, send follow-up email same day.
D8
Email 3: Value drop — "3 most common MuleSoft implementation mistakes." No hard ask. Soft CTA with Calendly link.
D10
LinkedIn DM: After connection accepted. Short, casual — references email thread.
D13
Email 4 (highest reply rate): "Your MuleSoft team asked us to reach out." Co-sell angle. Only used when VP Alliances has AE's approval to reference them. Reply rate: 8–12%.
D16
Final call: "I won't keep calling after this." Respects time — gets callbacks.
D18
Break-up email: "Closing the loop — I'll stop following up." Generates 15–25% of all sequence replies.
Owner: SDR/BDR via Instantly → Full SDR/BDR Outbound Playbook
Step 3 — Qualifying Call (5 questions, <10 min)
Identify pain bucket before booking 3-way call
When prospect replies with interest, SDR runs a quick qualifying call to identify which of the 4 pain buckets they're in — so the Alliance Mgr/AE and MuleSoft AE walk into the 3-way already knowing what to say.
Q1
Bucket A — Licence waste: Bought MuleSoft/Salesforce, not deployed or underutilised. Pitch: speed to ROI.
Q2
Bucket B — Bad implementation: Previous SI or internal build is broken. Pitch: rescue and rebuild.
Q3
Bucket C — Integration build: Systems don't talk. Manual work, data silos. Pitch: greenfield integration build.
Q4
Bucket D — Capacity: Engineering team overwhelmed, backlog 3+ months. Pitch: capacity extension.
Owner: SDR/BDR Before every booking
Step 4 — 3-Way Call Booking & Handoff
Prospect + DataSkate + MuleSoft AE on one call
SDR books the call, sends a 1-page prospect brief to Alliance Mgr/AE and MuleSoft AE, then steps out. The brief includes: pain bucket, exact words prospect used, tech stack, decision-maker confirmation, and suggested opening line for the AE.
1
SDR books 3-way via Calendly — prospect selects time, invites sent to Alliance Mgr/AE and MuleSoft AE automatically.
2
SDR sends 1-page brief via Slack to Alliance Mgr/AE and MuleSoft AE before call: pain bucket, exact prospect quotes, tech stack, authority confirmed, suggested opener.
3
Alliance Mgr/AE runs the discovery. MuleSoft AE adds platform credibility. SDR does NOT attend — their job is done at booking.
4
Post-call: Alliance Mgr/AE sends 2-line outcome to SDR — converted/not, next steps. SDR logs in HubSpot and updates sequence status.

Motion 3 — AE Named Account Data from Partner Enablement
The MuleSoft AE shares their named account list. The SDR/BDR pre-researches each account and hands it back to the AE as a gift — pain identified, decision-maker found, outreach drafted. The AE gets a ready-to-call list. DataSkate gets a co-sell opportunity.
📋
Step 1 — Clean the list
Remove accounts that are too small (below $5K SOW potential), wrong industry, or already a DS client. Score each remaining account on: platform fit, company size, and integration complexity signal.
Output: scored prospect list per AE
🔍
Step 2 — Pre-research each account
For each account in the AE's territory: find the pain (job postings, LinkedIn, press), identify the decision-maker, and write 2 sentences on why DataSkate is relevant to them right now.
Output: 1-page prospect profile per account
🎁
Step 3 — Deliver as a gift to the AE
"Here are 10 accounts in your territory. I've done the research — here's the pain, the contact, and why they need MuleSoft + DS right now. Pick the 2 you want to work." No ask. Pure give.
Output: AE receives ready-to-call accounts
📞
Step 4 — Joint outreach
Offer to co-author the outreach email with the AE. AE sends it — their name, their relationship. DS is mentioned as the implementation partner. AE gets the credit, DS gets the deal.
Output: Outreach sent, prospect engaged
🤝
Step 5 — DS leads discovery
When prospect responds, DS runs the discovery call. AE attends. DS qualifies against BANT, identifies the SOW scope, and sends the AE a deal summary within 24 hours of the call.
Output: Qualified deal in CRM
📊
Step 6 — Track and report
Log every account in CRM with source = "Salesforce Enablement Data." Track which AE sourced it. Send a monthly report to the VP Alliances — conversion rate, deal size, pipeline value by AE.

Motion 3 — Partner Events & Webinars
Events are where AEs and prospects self-identify. The goal is not branding — it's 5 warm contacts per event who become either prospects or AE relationships. Every event must have a post-event action plan before you attend.
Before the event
Pre-event prep (Must do)
Never attend an event without a target list and a leave-behind.
1
Pull attendee list (if available) or speaker list. Identify 5 target contacts — AEs, CAMs, or prospects. Research each one before the event.
2
Prepare one leave-behind: a QR code linking to your best recorded webinar on the capability the event is focused on. Not a brochure.
3
Set a clear success metric before attending: "I will leave with 5 LinkedIn connections and 2 follow-up meeting bookings."
Owner: Alliance Mgr/AE 1 week before
At the event
What to do in the room
Conversations, not pitches. Your goal is to learn their problem, not explain your product.
1
Opening line to any AE: "What are your hardest accounts to close right now?" Listen first. Offer the prospect profile or webinar link as a tool they can use.
2
Opening line to any prospect: "What integration or data challenge is your team working through?" Two minutes of listening gives you everything you need for the follow-up.
3
Book the follow-up in the room, not via email later. "Can we get 30 minutes next week? I have something specific for your situation." Calendar invite before you leave the conversation.
Owner: Alliance Mgr/AE Day of event
After the event
Post-event action within 24 hours
Most of the value from an event is lost if you don't act within 24 hours.
1
Log all contacts in CRM — source = event name. Connect on LinkedIn with a personalised note referencing the conversation. Do this the same night.
2
Send follow-up emails to booked meetings — include the specific webinar link relevant to their problem. Not a generic brochure.
3
Report to VP Alliances: how many contacts, how many meetings booked, estimated pipeline value, any AE relationships initiated.
Owner: Alliance Mgr/AE Within 24hrs
Speaking slots
Apply to speak — every event where possible
Speaking is 10x more effective than attending. A 15-minute slot gives you inbound conversations for the rest of the event.
1
Pitch topic: "How [Company Type] cut integration time by 40% using MuleSoft + [DS accelerator]." Real client result, specific to the audience.
2
Use the recorded webinar library as proof of capability when applying. Link to your best webinar as a sample of your speaking quality.
3
GTM Manager submits speaker applications 6 weeks ahead. VP Alliances approves topic. CEO attends for major Salesforce or MuleSoft events.
Owner: GTM Manager submits · VP Alliances approves

Motion 5 — Free Recorded Webinar Library for AEs
AEs are trying to sell capabilities they don't deeply understand. You give them the tool to do it — a library of short recorded webinars on the exact use cases their prospects ask about. AEs share these to warm cold prospects without calling you. This is the most scalable inbound motion.
What makes a good webinar in this library
10–15 minutes max. AEs won't watch 45 minutes. Prospects won't either.
Use-case specific, not product generic. "How to integrate Salesforce and SAP in 4 weeks" beats "MuleSoft integration overview."
Real client outcome in the title. AEs share content that makes them look smart. A specific result does that.
Ends with a clear CTA. "Book a free 30-minute scoping call" with a link. Not a contact form. A direct calendar link.
Gated with email capture. Viewer submits email to watch → goes into DS CRM as a warm inbound lead → Alliance Mgr/AE follows up within 24hrs.
Priority topics — what AEs need most
MuleSoft: "How to migrate from point-to-point integrations to API-led connectivity in 4 weeks"
MuleSoft: "Salesforce + ERP integration — the 3 most common failures and how to avoid them"
Salesforce: "Setting up Agentforce for a 50-person sales team in 3 weeks"
Salesforce: "Sales Cloud implementation — what SMBs get wrong and how to fix it"
Informatica: "How to clean and unify your customer data before a Salesforce go-live"
Workato: "Automating your quote-to-cash process — live demo in 12 minutes"
Production process — 1 new video every 2 weeks
Week 1, Day 1: VP Alliances picks topic based on what AEs are currently asking about. Practice Lead is briefed.
Week 1, Day 3: Practice Lead records a raw walkthrough — screen share + voice. No studio. Loom or similar.
Week 1, Day 5: GTM Manager edits, adds intro slide and CTA slide, uploads to gated landing page.
Week 2, Day 1: Alliance Mgr/AE sends link to all Tier A and B AEs with a 2-line note: "Here's a 12-min walkthrough on [topic]. Your prospects who ask about this can watch it before your call."
Ongoing: GTM Manager tracks views, email captures, and inbound requests from each video. Reports monthly to VP Alliances.
How AEs use the library — the script
AE is on a prospect call. Prospect asks: "How long does MuleSoft integration actually take?" AE says: "I'll send you a 12-minute walkthrough from our implementation partner — they've done this 20+ times."
AE sends the link after the call. Prospect watches. Prospect submits email to access. DS CRM gets the lead. Alliance Mgr/AE follows up within 24 hours.
DS informs AE: "Your prospect just watched the MuleSoft video — we're following up. Want to join the discovery call?" AE is now re-engaged without any extra effort.
This makes the AE look credible to their prospect, requires zero effort from the AE, and puts DS in front of a warm lead. That's the flywheel.

AE 3-Week Enablement Structure
Any AE who engages with DataSkate should be fully enabled to pitch, qualify, and intro DS to prospects within 3 weeks — without calling you for help. This is the standard. Build the kit once, reuse it for every AE.

MustSend the AE the Enablement Kit: 1-page pitch card per use case, 5-10 pre-researched prospects in their territory, objection cheat sheet, webinar library link.

GoodAdd a co-branded one-pager the AE can forward to prospects under their own name. Their brand, DS capability.

What keeps it to 4 weeks — the non-negotiables
✓
Scope locked at SOW sign — any addition becomes a new SOW. No scope creep inside the 4-week window, no exceptions.
✓
Practice Lead assigned before SOW is signed — not after. No deal starts without a named resource confirmed.
✓
Client SME availability is a SOW condition. If the client misses their 3hrs/week obligation, the delivery clock pauses on their side — not DS's.
✓
Delivery Head/SA approves the architecture before kickoff. No surprises in Week 3.
✓
Environment access confirmed on Day 1. If access is not received in 3 business days of kickoff, PM escalates to client sponsor — not AE.
✕
No delivery work begins before SOW is countersigned. Even if the client says "just get started." This is a hard rule — no exceptions.
